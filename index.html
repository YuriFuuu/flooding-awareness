Flood Awareness: Understand the Impact of America’s Most Frequent Natural Disaster Introduction


Introduction


Flooding is the most common and costly natural disaster in the United States. Each year, severe weather events like hurricanes, heavy rainfall, and rising sea levels cause billions of dollars in damage and disrupt countless lives. Are you prepared for the next flood?


This page highlights the financial and personal impacts of flooding, shares lessons from past disasters, and emphasizes the importance of preparedness. Explore the data, stories, and insights below to deepen your understanding and take steps to protect yourself and your community

Lessons from Recent Flooding Events

Flooding is often a result of severe storms, which are becoming more frequent and intense. In 2024, Hurricanes Helene and Milton caused widespread flooding and devastation across Florida, highlighting the need for better preparedness.


Hurricane Helene: Landfall on September 24, 2024, as a Category 4 storm in Florida’s Big Bend region.

Hurricane Milton: Struck Florida on October 9, 2024, as a Category 3 storm, compounding the damage from Helene.


View detailed maps and paths of Hurricanes Helene and Milton to understand how storms create flooding risks.

Hurricane Helene, a Category 4 storm at landfall, caused widespread devastation from Florida's Gulf Coast to Tennessee, leaving over 600 miles of destruction and record flooding. By Sunday, officials reported more than 100 fatalities due to the storm.

In Western North Carolina, Hurricane Helene's devastation is jeopardizing access to health care for many, including Tina Abbott, who was already managing multiple medical issues. She faces pain from a tissue tear, a spinal cyst, and chronic respiratory problems requiring a portable oxygen tank—so essential she had to refinance her car to pay for it.

In Erwin, Tennessee, rescue workers have recovered the body of the final person still missing after massive flooding from Helene hit a plastic factory there. Rosa Andrade, 29, was one of six employees killed after they were unable to escape the rising waters around Impact Plastics in Erwin, a small town in East Tennessee. Surviving workers have stated they were not allowed to leave until water had flooded the plant’s parking lot and the power went out. Eleven people were swept away and only five were rescued.

Ariel and Tiffany Lopez, residents of Shore Acres in St. Petersburg, Florida, underestimated Hurricane Helene, expecting only four inches of floodwater like Hurricane Idalia. However, they encountered a sudden surge that rose to four feet, forcing them to swim to safety with their four children. After reaching a friend's house, they returned to rescue their five pets. This terrifying experience led the couple to decide to move inland to avoid future flooding, viewing it as the final push they needed for a change.

As Hurricane Milton nears Florida, officials urge early evacuations in vulnerable areas due to the storm's uncertain path. Florida's emergency management director advised residents to leave Monday, while Gov. Ron DeSantis emphasized widespread evacuations, recalling the deadly impact of Hurricane Ian in 2022.
